# Preserve Our Strengths

Date: 2026-03-21

## Strengths That Must Not Be Lost

### 1. Strict validation gates

Why it matters:

- prevents fantasy listings
- protects margin truth
- keeps unsafe supplier records from reaching marketplaces

Why it must not be lost:

- competitors often optimize for convenience first; we already optimize for truth first

Build-on rule:

- future supplier expansion must feed this gate, not bypass it

### 2. Cancellation truth and exclusion of false commercial proof

Why it matters:

- protects business reporting from inflated success claims

Why it must not be lost:

- a cancelled or manually invalidated order must never count as a real sale

Build-on rule:

- any new dashboards or reports must respect commercial-proof eligibility

### 3. Fee completeness logic

Why it matters:

- real margin depends on costs, marketplace fees, payment fees, shipping, and taxes

Why it must not be lost:

- weak competitor patterns often stop at buy-price versus sell-price

Build-on rule:

- new pricing automation must preserve fee completeness before publication

### 4. Webhook proof granularity

Why it matters:

- distinguishing stored verification, live readiness, and inbound-event proof keeps the team honest

Why it must not be lost:

- blending historical proof with current readiness creates false confidence

Build-on rule:

- future webhook reporting must show both stored proof and current live state

### 5. Auditable state transitions

Why it matters:

- without state auditability, first-profit debugging becomes guesswork

Why it must not be lost:

- the final transformation needs more observability, not less

Build-on rule:

- each new automation stage should leave explicit state evidence

### 6. Conservative realized-profit engine

Why it matters:

- it refuses to count demo or invalid rows as success

Why it must not be lost:

- once real profit starts flowing, this engine becomes the truth source

Build-on rule:

- add richer input data, but do not weaken exclusion rules

### 7. Fulfillment orchestration with retry and manual fallback

Why it matters:

- real operations need controlled retries and explicit human escalation

Why it must not be lost:

- a failed purchase should move into a truth-preserving queue, not disappear

Build-on rule:

- competitor-inspired automation should plug into our manual queue instead of replacing it with silent failure
